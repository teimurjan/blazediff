Designing for Failure: A Resilience Playbook

April 2, 2026 · Devon Park

Staff Engineer, Platform

Every system fails. The question is never whether but how gracefully — and whether your users ever notice.

We rebuilt our payment gateway around the assumption that any dependency can vanish at any moment. Circuit breakers, bulkheads, and aggressive timeouts became the default, not the exception.

The result was counterintuitive: by planning for collapse, we shipped faster. Engineers stopped fearing deploys because the blast radius of any single fault was provably contained.

"Resilience isn't the absence of failure. It's the presence of recovery."
