The Quiet Return of the Urban Forest

By Helena Or — March 14, 2026 · 8 min read

In cities long defined by concrete, a slow green revolution is underway. Reclaimed lots and forgotten rail corridors are being stitched back into living canopy, one sapling at a time.

Researchers tracking microclimates report that even modest tree cover can lower surface temperatures by several degrees, offering relief during the longer, fiercer summers that now define the region.
